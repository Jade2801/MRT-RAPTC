Resources

This is where you will find any resources that we think might be useful to get a better understand of MRT or resources we have written:

  • Mental Resilience Training review
    This is a 12000 word review that was written by us to get a better understanding of MRT in the RAPTC, we read more than 100 papers. You will be able to find a reference list at the end of this review if you're interested in reading more papers. It is a comprehensive review looking at MRT within elite sport and military contexts and finally putting it togther to look at Mental Resilience Training and Psychological Skills Training in the RAPTC. It's a long read and most of it has been summarised in this website so this is not a necessary read but it will give you more of in depth grasp of MRT.
  • Mental Resilience Training crib cards
    These crib cards were created as a way to remember what each pillar of MRT is and what they look like in a session. This powerpoint version will allow you to reword or change some things to make it easier to use for you.
  • Mental Resilience Training audit
    We are carrying out an audit in order to understand which pillars are already being used during training sessions and which ones aren't. This will not only allow us to prove that MRT is already being used in PT sessions but also to identify the training gap and which pillars should be taught in more depth in order to be implemented into training sessions.
    This form is available on any device and it would be great if PTIs could fill them during their sessions. If you have any questions about it please send us an email.
  • Mental Resilience working group
    We have a working group in place with both military personel and experts in psychology in order to understand MRT better, share our ideas, knowledge and experiences. This working group meets every two weeks on Tuesdays through zoom. If you would like to be invovled because MRT is of particular interest to you, please send us an email.
